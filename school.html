The Science Museum has free activities suited for pre-schools and schools alike. Entrance, tours and activities are always free for Kindergarten and Schools.

Here you can find a list of popular learning activities, for booking, or if you have questions, or want a tailored learning experience, use the contact form below.

Orbital mechanics

This hands-on experience lets students explore the fascinating world of orbital mechanics. They'll gain an intuitive understanding of the forces that govern the movement of celestial bodies. The highlight? A chance to pilot a spaceship in a state-of-the-art simulator, attempting a challenging landing manoeuvre. It's a fun, engaging way to bring physics to life!

Nuclear Energy

Students will delve into the principles of nuclear reactions and their applications in energy generation. The highlight of the session? A chance to operate a simulated nuclear reactor, where they get to control the reaction rate and safety systems. It's an exciting, hands-on approach to demystify the complex field of nuclear energy!

Lab science

This interactive journey immerses students in the captivating realm of chemistry. They'll gain insights into chemical reactions, molecular structures, and the periodic table. The highlight of the experience? A chance to mix safe chemicals under expert supervision, causing colorful, exciting reactions. It's a thrilling, hands-on way to make the intricate world of chemistry tangible and fun!

The Industrial Revolution

This guided tour is specifically crafted for school classes, offering an enlightening journey through the pivotal Industrial Revolution era. Students will learn about the breakthroughs in machinery, transportation, and manufacturing that fuelled rapid societal changes.
